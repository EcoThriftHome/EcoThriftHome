12 Free Habits That Lower Your Energy Bill Starting Tonight

These zero-cost behavioral changes can save the average household $150–$250 per year. No purchases required.

💡

Turn Off Lights When Leaving a Room

Simple but powerful — lighting accounts for ~15% of a home's electricity. Saves approximately $30–$50/year.

❄️

Lower Your Thermostat 7–10°F When Away or Asleep

ENERGY STAR recommends 68°F when awake, 60°F when asleep or away in winter. Saves up to $180/year.

🫧

Wash Clothes in Cold Water

90% of a washing machine's energy goes to heating water. Cold water cleans just as effectively. Saves approximately $40/year.

🔌

Unplug Chargers and Electronics When Not in Use

Chargers, TVs, and game consoles draw power 24/7 even when idle. Saves approximately $100–$150/year.

🌞

Use Natural Light During the Day

Open blinds and curtains before turning on lights. Saves approximately $20–$40/year on lighting.

🍽️

Run Your Dishwasher Only When Full

A half-full dishwasher uses the same energy as a full one. Air-dry instead of heat-dry. Saves approximately $25/year.

🚿

Take Shorter Showers

Cutting a 10-minute shower to 5 minutes reduces hot water heating costs significantly. Saves approximately $50–$75/year.

🌬️

Use Fans Instead of AC When Possible

A ceiling fan costs about $0.01/hour to run vs $0.36/hour for central AC. Saves approximately $60–$100/year.

🧺

Air-Dry Clothes When Weather Allows

A dryer is one of the most energy-intensive appliances. Air-drying even once a week saves approximately $25–$40/year.

🫙

Keep Your Refrigerator Full (But Not Overpacked)

A full fridge retains cold better and requires less energy to stay cool. Use water jugs to fill empty space. Saves approximately $15–$25/year.

🪟

Close Curtains on Hot Summer Days

Blocking direct sunlight reduces heat gain and AC load. Saves approximately $30–$50/year in summer.

🧹

Clean or Replace HVAC Filters Monthly

A dirty filter forces your HVAC to work harder, increasing energy use by 5–15%. Saves approximately $30–$60/year.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions about cutting energy bills on a budget.

Most households can save between $300–$600 per year by combining a few key strategies: switching to LED bulbs, installing a smart thermostat, eliminating vampire power, sealing drafts, and adopting better habits. The exact amount depends on your current energy use, home size, and climate — but the changes we cover are consistently among the highest-ROI improvements available.

The cheapest improvements are entirely free behavioral changes: lowering your thermostat when you leave the house, unplugging devices on standby, washing clothes in cold water, and using fans instead of air conditioning. These habits alone can save $100–$200/year with zero upfront cost.

Yes — LEDs are one of the highest-ROI energy upgrades available. They use 75–80% less electricity than incandescent bulbs and last 10–25 times longer. The average household that replaces all its incandescent bulbs with LEDs saves $100–$200 per year on electricity, while the bulbs themselves typically cost $1–$2 each and last 10+ years.

Vampire power (also called standby power or phantom load) is the electricity devices consume even when switched off or in standby mode. Common culprits include TVs, cable boxes, game consoles, phone chargers, microwaves, and desktop computers. According to the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, the average US household wastes $100–$200 per year on vampire power — roughly 10% of total electricity use.
