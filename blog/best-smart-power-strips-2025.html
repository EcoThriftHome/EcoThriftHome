Best Smart Power Strips in 2025: 4 Options Ranked for Energy Savings

Not all smart power strips are created equal. We break down the four best options — from the top app-controlled pick to the best no-WiFi set-and-forget strip — so you can eliminate phantom power without overspending.

Your home entertainment center. Your home office. Your bedroom TV setup. Each one is quietly drawing electricity right now — even though nothing is actively in use. This invisible drain, called phantom power or standby power, costs the average U.S. household $100–$200 per year according to the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory.

A smart power strip is the most effective tool for eliminating it. But with dozens of options available, choosing the right one comes down to a few key questions: Do you want app control and scheduling? Or do you just want something that works automatically without any setup? Do you have a home office, an entertainment center, or both? The right answer depends on your situation — and that's exactly what this guide is here to settle.

Bottom Line Up Front: The Kasa HS300 is the best smart power strip for most households. The Smart Strip ECG-7MVR is the best pick if you want zero setup and no WiFi required. Read on for the full breakdown of all four options.

How We Evaluated These Strips

We evaluated each strip across five criteria that matter most for energy saving: phantom power elimination effectiveness, ease of setup, outlet layout and flexibility, surge protection rating, and overall value relative to price. We also factored in whether each strip works without WiFi or a smart home hub, since not everyone wants another app on their phone.

Full Reviews

🥇 Kasa Smart Power Strip HS300 — Best Overall

The Kasa HS300 is the clear winner for most households and the strip we recommend first. Its six outlets are individually controllable from the Kasa app — meaning you can turn any single outlet on or off remotely, set different schedules for each one, and monitor exactly how many watts each device is drawing in real time. That last feature is genuinely useful: plug in your cable box, check the app, and see exactly what it's costing you in standby. If you want to go deeper on tracking consumption across your whole home, a dedicated power usage monitor can give you a whole-home view to complement per-outlet data. The HS300 works with both Alexa and Google Home, requires no hub, and connects to any 2.4GHz WiFi network. Three always-on USB ports handle phone and tablet charging without occupying an outlet. Build quality is solid — it's ETL certified and includes 500-joule surge protection. The one limitation: it requires WiFi and the Kasa app to unlock its full feature set. If you lose internet or your phone dies, the outlets still work but you lose remote control and scheduling.

🥈 Smart Strip ECG-7MVR — Best No-WiFi Option

If you don't want an app, don't want to deal with WiFi setup, and just want something that works automatically from the moment you plug it in — the ECG-7MVR is exactly that. It uses current-sensing technology to detect when the device plugged into its control outlet (your TV or computer) powers off, then automatically cuts power to all four switched outlets. No scheduling. No smartphone. No internet connection. Plug your TV into the control outlet, plug your cable box, soundbar, and gaming console into the switched outlets, plug your router into one of the two always-on outlets, and walk away. From that point on, every time you turn your TV off, everything else cuts power automatically. Keep in mind that small devices like phone chargers also draw standby power and are worth plugging into a switched outlet when possible. The ECG-7MVR has been around for years and has a strong track record — it's a perennial Amazon bestseller with over 850 reviews. The limitation is obvious: no remote control, no energy monitoring, no scheduling beyond the basic master/switched logic.
